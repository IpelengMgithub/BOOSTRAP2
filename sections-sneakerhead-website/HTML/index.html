History behind the sneaker

the beginning

The term “Sneaker” was coined shortly after the release of the original Keds sneakers by advertising agent Henry Nelson Mckinney. The shoe’s rubber sole muffled the sounds of footsteps, making them almost silent, also making it a whole lot easier to ‘sneak’ up on someone – hence the word ‘sneakers’ was born.

marquis converse

By 1917 Marquis Converse produced the first sole made just for Basketball, called Converse All-Stars. A basketball superstar from Indiana named Chuck Taylor endorsed these sneakers and the Chuck Taylor All-Stars became the best-selling basketball shoes of all time.

adi dassler

Sneakers went international in the 1920s when a German man by the name of Adi Dassler, created the sneaker brand: Adidas, named after himself. The brand then became the most popular athletic shoe in the world. Adidas introduced leather and suede into the marketplace in the early 1970s. When pro basketball players started wearing them, an entirely new world of footwear was born.

bill bowerman

In 1971 Bill Bowerman, the esteemed creator of Nike, had a breakthrough moment one morning when he realized that his wife’s waffle iron would make the perfect tread he had envisioned for Nike’s first pair of running shoes. He was developing sneakers for his athletes that had the perfect grip for any surface.

up coming brands

The early 80s saw the emergence of brands such as Reebok and Puma (started by Adi Dassler’s Brother Rudi) but the true watershed moments came in the mid-1980s when the first Air Jordan’s dropped – considered the most world-renowned, iconic and timeless sneakers ever made to date

sneaker and streatwear industry

The boom of signature kicks in this era provided the necessary elements to birth a community where the goal was to wear the freshest, hardest to find, limited edition sneakers that have grown to an almost mythic proportion. What once began as a sub-culture is now the omnipresent global, multi-billion dollar sneaker and streetwear industry.
